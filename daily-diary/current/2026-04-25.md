## Main Topics

1. **OCR pipeline debugging — iterative diagnosis**: Continued from previous session where the new projection-based `ocr_expression()` was written but untested. Ran `test_ocr.py` multiple times, reading debug images each round to isolate bugs.

2. **Split bug — dead space as operator**: The expression text occupies only the right portion of the EXPR_REGION crop. The leading dead space created a 165px "valley" that was picked as the operator instead of the real digit-operator gap. Fixed by finding the largest contiguous activity island first, then running valley detection within that island only.

3. **"+" operator double-valley bug**: The "+" operator creates two narrow gaps (before and after its vertical stroke) separated by ~20px of content. The valley detection was picking only the first gap, leaving the second half of "+" in the right digit region. Fixed by merging nearby valley runs with `merge_gap=30` — merges the "+" sub-valleys into one while leaving the ~37px trailing gap after narrow digits (like "1") unmerged.

4. **"1" trailing space merge bug**: With `merge_gap=40`, the gap between the end of narrow digit "1" (~37px) and the trailing island edge was being merged into the operator valley, consuming the right digit entirely (right_g = 1px). Reducing to `merge_gap=30` fixed this.

5. **OCR inversion bug**: The `_ocr_digit_gray()` function was applying `cv2.bitwise_not()` after threshold. Image inspection confirmed that the near-white dialog background (~235+) is ABOVE 225 and the character pixels (both outline and interior at 5x scale) are BELOW 225. So `THRESH_BINARY(225)` already gives black-on-white — the correct format for Tesseract. `bitwise_not` was flipping it the wrong way, producing white-on-black which Tesseract can't read. Removed the NOT.
